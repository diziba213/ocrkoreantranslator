## Case Overview (HBS 9-803-045)

**Company:** Athleta Corporation — a women's multi-channel sports apparel company (catalog, online, retail) founded by Scott Kerslake, headquartered in Petaluma, CA.

**Situation (early 2002):** Athleta needs a **Series E round** of ~$3.43M to fund growth into physical retail. The company had previously raised Series A–D from VC firms. The dot-com bust made VC markets very difficult in 2002.

**Financials (FY ending May 31):**
- Net Sales: $1.39M (1999) → $7.7M (2000) → $14.3M (2001)
- Operating losses throughout; cumulative retained deficit of ~$3.41M by 2001
- Gross margin ~43%, but heavy marketing/catalog spend kept EBIT negative

**Use of Series E proceeds (~$3.43M total):**
- Catalog circulation growth: $1.48M
- Supplier terms improvement: $0.68M
- Infrastructure/distribution center upgrade: $0.58M
- Human resources: $0.22M
- Merchandising/store expansion/marketing: $0.47M

**Competitive position:** Quadrant 1 (Women's Sports Retailers) alongside Title 9 Sports; differentiated by multi-channel model, private-label brand, and high-quality customer service culture.

## Key Analytical Dimensions

**Financing options available:**
- **VCs:** Difficult post-dot-com; consumer retail VCs had exited the market; required strong growth story and exit path
- **Angel investors:** Available but limited to $750K–$2M; preferred "partner" relationships over pure financial returns; typical check $750K–$1M
- **Commercial banks:** Very tight-fisted in 2002; could provide small bridge debt

**Valuation factors:** Revenue growth trajectory, gross margin (~43%), path to profitability, market size (women's sports apparel ~$6B+, participation growing), brand differentiation, management team depth, exit comparables, and post-dot-com risk appetite.

**Term sheet considerations (investor perspective):** Liquidation preference, anti-dilution provisions, board composition, pro-rata rights, vesting schedules, use-of-proceeds restrictions, and milestones tied to tranched funding.
